The Cast

Natasha Perry-Fagant

MOON

In addition to her training at Concordia University for her B.F.A. Theatre Performance Specialization, Natasha’s impressively long list of skills include Stage Combat and, having studied Chinese Opera at the National Academy of Chinese Theatre Arts, she is also proficient in Long Silk Dancing, Fan Dancing, Water Sleeves, and Jin and Dao Swords.

Alycia Duncan

Woman

Originally from the Vancouver area, Alycia has spent the last year studying in the theatre performance program at Concordia. Aside from studying theatre, she has had the privilege to be a part of many opportunities such as Concordia’s Vagina Monologues where she performed at Cafe Cleopatra. She looks forward to her future endeavours as she begins her career here in Montreal.

Rémi Mireault

MAN

Going into his second year at Concordia, Rémi has regularly been a part of theatre productions since the age of 3. Though mostly on a level of community theatre, occasionally professional as well. This has developed in him a keen interest in collaborative projects such as this one.

Nadine Cayer

NARRATOR, SHAMAN

Nadine studied at the Mtl. School Of Performing Arts, Studio 303, Tom Todoroff Studio, and the Ottawa Theatre School Professional Conservatory Program. She has also trained in fencing and synchronized swimming.

Nic Turcotte

COMIC SAILOR

Nic has been creating in Montreal for a while now and intends to continue doing so for the forseeable future. Thus far, he has studied theatre at Concordia University and John Abbott College and is excited to continue learning, training, and creating beside all the wonderful artists he has encountered thus far and continues to meet all the time in this wonderful city.

David Hall

BURLY SAILOR

David Hall is a Montreal based actor who first started performing with Young Actors for Young Audiences at the Segal Center as part of the Yiddish Theatre festival. He graduated Dawson College’s Professional Theatre program with honors in 2013 and is currently going into his second year at Concordia’s Theatre and Development program. He is excited to be a part of his first Fringe festival and hopes to be a part of many more.
